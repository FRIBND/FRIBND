Signature Quantum Number and Splitting

1. Definitions

Signature (α\alphaα) defines the wave function symmetry of an axially deformed nucleus under a 180∘180^\circ180∘ rotation (RxR_xRx​) about an axis perpendicular to the symmetry axis.

  • Operator: Rx(π)=e−iπJxR_x(\pi) = e^{-i\pi J_x}Rx​(π)=e−iπJx​
  • Eigenvalue: r=e−iπαr = e^{-i\pi \alpha}r=e−iπα

2. Signature Splitting

The Coriolis interaction acts on high-jjj valence particles (intruder orbitals), splitting the rotational band (ΔI=1\Delta I = 1ΔI=1) into two branches (ΔI=2\Delta I = 2ΔI=2) with different energies.

  • Observation: Energy level staggering (odd-even shift).
  • Mechanism: An energy term ∝(−1)I+K\propto (-1)^{I+K}∝(−1)I+K shifts the favored branch lower and the unfavored branch higher.
  • Signature Inversion: Anomaly where the theoretically unfavored branch is lower in energy at low spins.
